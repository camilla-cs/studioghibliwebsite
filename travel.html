Travel spots that inspired Ghibli's movies:

In Hayao Miyazaki's movies he takes inspiration from various locations, first of all Japan.

Howl's Moving Castle 🍦🔖

Colmar (France) is said to be the main inspiration for Studio Ghibli's "Howl's Moving Castle". The town is characterized by pastel colored houses bracketed by wooden beams, a canal running through the streets and flowers. Additionally, Copenhagen and the English town of Cornwall are also reminescents of the movie's locations.

Spirited Away 🐉🚃

The first thing that comes to mind when thinking of "Spirited Away" is the grandiose red bathhouse, where the majority of the story takes place. It was inspired by two locations: the Dogo Onsen Bath House in Matsuyama (Japan) with its luxurius interior, and the second being the town of Jiufen in Taiwan.
Dogo onsen official website Jiufen 九份: The Beautiful Taiwanese Mountain Town

Princess Mononoke 🐺🏹

The thick forests populated by scary but also adorable creatures is inspired by Yakushima Island in Japan, which is popular among locals for the enchanting forest that is part of the island. Moreover, the thatched-roofed houses of Shirakawa go might have inspired the film's Iron town.
Yakushima Island Shirakawa go

My Neighbour Totoro

In contrast to the wild and dangerous nature in "Princess Monoke", the rice fields in "My Neighbour Totoro" are calm and sweet. Here we find two sister giggling around their backyard and stumbling upon a fluffy Totoro. The setting of the movie is inspired by Sayama Hills (Japan).
Sayama Hills

Kiki's Delivery Service 🎀🧹

Now we head towards northen Europe where it takes place the location of my favourite Ghibli's movie: "Kiki's Delivery Service". The town that inspired Miyazaki and his team is Visby, in Sweden, a port city looking over the Baltic Sea. The town has many churches and hills.
Visby: Sweden's Fairytale town

Ponyo 🐙🌊

"Ponyo" , with its oceans and strange creatures is said to be inspired from a town named Tomonoura based in Hiroshima's Prefecture.
Tomonoura: Picturesque , old-fashioned fishing town

The Castle in the Sky 🏰🌈

"Miyazaki visited Wales for the first time in 1984" and witnessing firsthand the working class strike said "I admired the way they battled to save their way of life, just as the coal miners did in Japan." He was then inspired and depicted miners in his movie Laputa: Castle in the Sky as strong and courageous figures.

Moreover, the visit to Rhondda in South Wales is believed to be the primary source of inspiration for the background artists.
Hayao Miyazaki real life locations

Porco Rosso 🍝✈️

Marco Pagot also known as Porco Rosso ("Red Pig" in Italian) is a WWI fighter pilot who has been transformed into a pig due to a curse and he protects italian ships from pirates in the Adriatic sea. Porco's secret hideout is based on a cove in the Croatian Island of Vis.
